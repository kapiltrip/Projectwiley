A R T I C L E I N F O  
A B S T R A C T  
Due to the high cost of timing signoff in the routing stage of VLSI (very large-scale integration), timing analysis  
in the placement stage is usually used to assist the implementation. Still, uncertainty exists due to the lack of  
detailed wiring information. Considering the close relationship between signal propagation and circuit topology,  
we propose a machine-learning method to predict the circuit timing using complex network features and construct  
basic and augmented models for comparison. Firstly, in the placement stage, the fundamental circuit features  
related to timing optimization are extracted, and the complex network features on the timing path are computed  
by complex network modeling, based on which the dataset for machine learning is generated. Secondly, we  
use machine learning algorithms to compare the performance of the two models in predicting circuit timing.  
Experimental results show that the augmented model combining complex network features outperforms the basic  
model in timing prediction accuracy. The augmented model achieves a 12.5 % reduction in MSE (mean squared  
error) for predicting slack, reaching 0.004515. It also shows a 1.06 % improvement in ꢀ2, reaching 0.9314, and  
a 0.54 % improvement in ꢁ, reaching 0.9652. For predicting delay, the MSE is reduced by 11.69 %, reaching  
0.000302, while ꢀ2 improves by 0.03 % to 0.9973, and ꢁ improves by 0.02 % to 0.9987. The deviation in  
predicting WNS (worst negative slack) is reduced by 6.53 %, reaching 5.30 %, and for TNS (total negative slack),  
it is reduced by 1.59 %, reaching 12.38 %. The AUC (area under the ROC (receiver operating characteristic)  
curve) value for identifying critical paths increases by 0.26 %, reaching 0.9873. These results demonstrate the  
efficiency of the proposed method in utilizing complex network features for predicting circuit timing.

1. Introduction  
Designers employ timing analysis to evaluate circuit timing per-  
formance, ensuring that the circuit’s timing behavior meets design  
Timing convergence in very large-scale integration (VLSI) design is  
one of the key challenges in achieving the expected operating frequen-  
cies and delay constraints in circuit requirements [1,2]. Static timing  
analysis (STA) is one of the most common techniques used in digital de-  
sign verification to resolve various timing issues and accelerate timing  
convergence in chip design tasks [3].  
requirements. The accuracy and efficiency of this process significantly  
impact chip quality and cost. If post-routing timing analysis reveals  
violations, designers must modify and optimize circuits until timing con-  
vergence is achieved. To conserve substantial design resources during  
the design process, timing analysis in the layout phase becomes critical.

Machine learning has made notable advancements in circuit timing

layout phase. Guo et al. [8] presented a heuristic-driven temporal engine  
graph neural network approach for predicting arrival times and Slack of

Complex networks, serving as mathematical tools to effectively

3. Using complex network features for timing prediction  
The basic circuit timing features used in this paper include the results  
of static timing analysis during the placement phase and detailed infor-  
mation about individual units. Table 1 displays the names, descriptions,  
and quantities of circuit timing features used in the experiments.  
In this work, we do not use cell delay and transition time correlated  
with transition rate as the features for the prediction. There are two main  
styles of timing analysis, i.e., graph-based timing analysis (GBA) mode  
and path-based timing analysis (PBA) mode. GBA mode can quickly ob-  
tain timing analysis results. However, the results are often pessimistic  
because it uses the worst signal transition time across all input pins of  
the cell to calculate cell delay. PBA uses the specific transition time of  
each pin to compute the cell delay and propagates the transition time  
along the path to obtain a relatively accurate delay for each multi-input  
stage. However, numerous data paths and complex calculations lead to  
a massive time overhead [17].  
This study uses the circuits from the ISPD 2016 benchmark [18] for  
the experiment. Combining integrated circuit design, complex network  
theory, and machine learning techniques through physical design, this  
study explores timing analysis and the correlation between complex net-  
work characteristics and circuit timing. This chapter provides a detailed  
introduction to the timing prediction method framework, complex net-  
work modeling, and the machine learning algorithms and evaluation  
metrics employed.

The second stage is model construction. In this paper, two models  
are proposed: the basic model and the augmented model. The dataset  
used to train the basic model includes only fundamental circuit features  
related to timing optimization. While these features play a role in pre-  
dicting circuit timing, they lack a comprehensive consideration of the  
circuit topology. Subsequently, the augmented model is constructed. In  
addition to the basic circuit features, this model’s dataset incorporates  
complex network features. A feature selection algorithm is used to se-

performance.

placement are extracted. Complex network modeling generates the com-  
plex network for the placement, from which complex network feature  
parameters such as degree DS, EC, BC, CC, and EBC are computed using  
the igraph package of R software.  
Finally, various machine-learning methods are applied to the  
datasets. The trained models are compared and analyzed using evalu-

efficiency of the proposed method for predicting circuit timing using  
complex network features.  
Subsequently, the circuit placement is routed using the Vivado [20]  
routing tool. Static timing analysis is performed on the circuit to gener-  
ate path-based timing reports. A timing path refers to the propagation  
path of a signal from a clock-triggered flip-flop to a target register in  
digital circuits consisting of a series of logic gates and wires. The work  
extracts timing path slacks and delays from post-routing timing reports,  
which serve as labels for the dataset. In addition, labels indicating crit-  
ical paths are generated based on positive and negative slacks. A path  
with negative slack is considered a critical path.  
3.2. Complex network modeling  
At the placement stage, we use complex network modeling to extract  
complex network features to construct the dataset. We extract mod-  
ule locations and module-to-module connection information from the  
placement file. This data forms the basis for creating a two-dimensional  
undirected weighted complex network. In the network representation,  
the circuit modules are represented as nodes, the connections are rep-  
resented as edges, and the half-perimeter distance between connected  
modules is the weight assigned to the edge.  
Finally, the obtained features and labels are matched according  
to each timing path, generating a data record for each path. The  
manipulation is repeated for all the circuits to generate the final dataset.

4.3. TNS and WNS prediction  
Worst negative slack (WNS) and total negative slack (TNS) are two  
critical metrics used in circuit timing analysis to evaluate the quality of  
timing constraints. WNS represents the least amount of negative slack  
among all timing paths. When slack is less than zero, it indicates a timing  
violation. Therefore, WNS represents the most severe instance of timing  
violations in the entire circuit. TNS, on the other hand, is the sum of the  
negative slack values across all timing paths, providing an assessment  
of the overall quality of the timing constraints.

5. Discussion  
To address the uncertainty issue in traditional methods for timing  
analysis during the placement phase of circuit design, this paper pro-  
poses a machine-learning approach that utilizes complex network fea-  
tures to predict post-routing circuit timing. Two models are constructed,  
including a basic model and an augmented model. Experimental re-  
sults demonstrate that the augmented model, incorporating complex  
network features, outperforms the basic model in timing prediction. It  
highlights the efficiency of complex network features in enhancing the  
performance of timing prediction models. This approach offers a novel  
perspective and method for timing optimization in the VLSI design field.  
It has been demonstrated that circuit features related to cell and path  
delay are significant in predicting the timing of analyses in machine  
learning models. The transition rate determines cell delay by altering  
transition time, which affects path delay due to uncertainty in arrival  
time. Therefore, cell delay and transition rate are mutually dependent  
factors in timing analysis.
