Timing analysis algorithm using a neural network under PVT variations

Yan Liu, Yan-Hua Ma *, Yu-Chun Chang **

School of Microelectronics, Dalian University of Technology, Dalian, China

A R T I C L E I N F O

A B S T R A C T

Keywords: PVT Neural network Viability analysis

Process-voltage-temperature variations result in an increase in logic gate delays, which further lead to timing violations. Because of the computational complexity of the operators such as max and sum in statistical static timing analysis, this paper proposes a timing analysis algorithm using neural network to evaluate circuit timing under the PVT variations. In the proposed algorithm, a neural network method is applied to model the mean or standard deviation of the gate delay. Then, a viability analysis method is introduced to recognize the true critical path. The simulation results on ISCAS89 benchmarks illustrate that compared with SPICE Monte Carlo simula tion, the error in the mean calculated by the proposed algorithm is less than 2%, and the error in the standard deviation is less than 3.64%. In addition, for s298, s344, s832, s1238 and s13207, the viability analysis algorithm achieves 1.04X, 1.01X, 2.77X, 1.62X and 4.48X speedup over full path search, respectively.

Our main contributions are summarized as follows.

With the size of transistors is continuously shrinking, the inevitable process variations that are the physical deviation of the parameters of transistors such as channel width and gate oxide thickness become more prominent [1]. The changes in physical parameters of transistors lead to the variations in circuit performance. Additionally, circuit performance is also affected by temperature and voltage [2]. The process-voltage-temperature variations are called the PVT effects. Because of these effects, the threshold voltage of transistors shifts, resulting in a change in logic gate delay, which eventually leads to the timing violation of the circuit [3]. Therefore, it is significant to evaluate the impact of PVT effects on circuit timing. Studies on the impact of PVT effects mainly concern the statistical static timing analysis (SSTA) based methods, which calculate the dis tribution function of circuit timing by propagating the hypothetical distribution function of logic gate delay [4,5]. The SSTA methods can be generally divided into block-based and path-based SSTA methods. The block-based SSTA method uses sum and max operators to propagate the delay distribution [6]. For simplifying the max operator, it is the Gaussian distribution that is often assumed to be the distribution of process parameters [3]. But practically, the Gaussian distribution is too ideal to calculate circuit timing accurately [7]. In Ref. 2. Timing analysis algorithm using a back-propagation neural network

2.2. Viability analysis

The overall framework of the proposed timing analysis algorithm consists of gate delay modeling and viability analysis method, as shown in Fig. 2. In the gate delay modeling stage, a neural network is applied to model the mean or standard deviation of the gate delay according to an appropriate number of SPICE Monte Carlo simulations. In the viability analysis stage, we first merge logic gates and their fan-outs into a graph; then all timing paths of the circuit can be found by a depth-first search algorithm. Next, based on the types of logic gates, the sensitivity con ditions of the paths could be constructed. Finally, the true critical paths can be recognized according to the sensitivity conditions.

According to Hrapcenko’s analysis, there may exist the false paths in circuit. Additionally, if the critical path in circuit is a false path, the estimated circuit timing is pessimistic. Therefore, it is very significant to identify the true critical path. To filter out the true critical path, we first collect all timing paths, the types of logic gates and the side input signals of the logic gates by the depth-first search algorithm. Subsequently, the sensitivity condition of the timing path is constructed. Thus, the true critical path can be recognized. An overview of the viability analysis is summarized in Al gorithm 1.

3. Simulation results

In this section, ISCAS85 benchmarks, ISCAS89 benchmarks [12] and the 130-nm process are applied to test our proposed algorithm. We implement our simulations on a Linux server with an Intel Xeon Plat inum CPU 8260L @2.4 GHz and 1T memory.

Sensitivity condition Since the signal change in the preceding logic gate would cause a change in the following logic gate, the combination of each logic gate’s side input signals constitutes the sensitivity conditions of the timing path. For p ∈L, according to logic gate gi on pathp, we can determine the types of gate gmn that connect to gi and are not on pathp, and then the signal of a gate gi input pin that is not on p is determined according to the input signal of the circuit. Based on these signal conditions, if the output of pathp is always 0, these conditions are called the sensitivity condi tions of pathp. Fig. 4 shows an example of a path sensitivity condition. 4. Conclusion

To address the PVT effects and the false paths problem, a timing analysis algorithm is proposed to evaluate circuit timing. We first construct a BPNN to model the mean or standard deviation of the logic gate delay. Then, the viability analysis is introduced to find the true critical path. The simulation results indicate that our algorithm can effectively predict the circuit timing when considering PVT effects and the false paths.
